Google Ads scale brief - pre-meeting read

Maria Nicola - scaling non-brand on Google

The goal: pull more new-customer volume out of Google and lean less on Meta. The data says it's there - two non-brand campaigns already win new customers for $40 and $86, versus roughly $100 on Meta - but both are capped at $10/day. Brand search, by contrast, is mostly existing customers and isn't the place to scale. This brief is the case and the action plan for today's meeting.

The opportunity

New-customer economics - where the scale is

This is the cut that matters. Google reports whether each sale came from a new or returning customer. Cost per new customer, last 90 days, by engine - benchmarked against the ~$100 Meta cost per acquisition. Two non-brand campaigns beat Meta comfortably and are both throttled at $10/day.

New share = new customers as a share of new + returning (Google's "unknown" bucket excluded). Read it with the cost: PMax AU Non-Brand books new customers at $40 with the cleanest attribution in the account (zero unknowns), and Shopping AU at $86 with 55% new. The brand and "Acquisition & Retention" Performance Max campaigns are 7-16% new - they harvest people who already know us.

On brand search and cannibalisation
Brand search is 84-98% returning or already-known customers, and a new customer off brand costs $185-286 - worse than Meta. A lot of that brand demand is people who saw a Meta ad, or word of mouth, and would likely find us organically. Brand spend isn't high and isn't the worry today, so leave it running as a cheap defensive floor - just don't pour scale budget into it. Confirming exactly how much is truly incremental (a brand-pause holdout test) is a second-priority follow-up, not today's focus.
Action plan

What to change - for today's meeting

Ordered by impact. The brief: move budget toward the engines that bring new customers under $100, and stop optimising them like retention. We can run new-customer campaigns down to 3-4x ROAS because the new cost still beats Meta well below that.

1
Uncap PMax AU Non-Brand Priority 1
$40 new-customer cost, 37% new, zero attribution noise - and it's sitting paused on a $10/day budget. Re-enable, lift budget to a real test level ($75-100/day), exclude existing-customer lists so it optimises for acquisition, and set a new-customer value rule so the system bids up for new buyers. Floor: 3-4x ROAS.
$40new-cust cost
2
Unthrottle Shopping AU Priority 1
$86 new-customer cost and 55% new, but strangled by a 1000% (10x) ROAS target on $10/day - which is why it loses 71% of visibility to ad rank, not budget. Drop the target to 400% (your 4x floor) and lift budget. Expect volume to jump at a still-sub-Meta cost.
$86new-cust cost
3
Launch non-brand search Priority 1
There is no non-brand search in the account - non-brand jewellery queries ("magnetic necklace clasps", "layered pearl necklace", "convertible necklace") only ever trigger through Shopping. Build one tightly-themed campaign around the convertible/magnetic necklace angle, exact and phrase match, tight negatives, new-customer value rule. This is the cleanest read on non-brand search demand and the most scalable long-term lever.
Testnew build
4
Split the "Acquisition & Retention" Performance Max campaigns Priority 2
CA-US and AU-NZ Collections are 7-10% new but their ROAS looks great because returning customers flatter it. Split each into a new-customer campaign (new-customer goal, existing lists excluded, bid up for new) and a retention one. That exposes the true acquisition cost and lets us scale the acquisition half on its own merits.
-restructure
5
Pause Demand Gen YouTube (AU + US) Priority 1
$1,891 over 90 days for 0.4 new customers between them. AU's new-customer cost is $2,355; US books zero. Pause both and move the ~$630/month into Priorities 1-3. Revisit Demand Gen later as a creative-led prospecting test, not an always-on line.
$630per month freed
6
Hold brand search, fix conversion goals Priority 3
Keep brand running as a cheap floor - don't scale it. Separately, Add to Cart and Begin Checkout are set as primary conversion actions. Value bidding currently keys to purchases so it's contained, but demote both to secondary so the new acquisition campaigns can't optimise toward carts.
-protect bidding
The budget swap, in one line
Free ~$630/month from Demand Gen, redirect it plus fresh budget into PMax Non-Brand, Shopping AU and new non-brand search. Hold brand. Measure on new-customer cost (target under $100, floor 3-4x ROAS), not blended ROAS - blended ROAS will fall as we buy more new customers, and that's the point.

Summary

The takeaway

The opportunity
New customers under $100
PMax AU Non-Brand ($40) and Shopping AU ($86) already beat Meta on new-customer cost and are both capped at $10/day. Uncap them, add non-brand search, optimise for new customers not blended ROAS. We can absorb 3-4x ROAS here because the new-customer cost still wins.
The trap to avoid
Scaling brand search
Brand is 84-98% existing customers at a $185-286 new-customer cost. It's a cheap defensive floor, not a growth lever. Hold it, don't feed it. Pause Demand Gen (0.4 new customers in 90 days) and move that budget to the engines that actually acquire.
